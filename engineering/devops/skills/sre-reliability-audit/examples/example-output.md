## TL;DR

Acme has a `runbooks/` directory with 14 documents, but only 4 are linked from paging alerts and 3 of the 14 haven't been updated in more than a year. There is no written SLO — uptime is a verbal "four nines" commitment but nothing is measured. Oncall is a single Slack channel where three engineers self-assign during outages. Postmortems exist for three incidents but use different templates and have no tracked action items. One game day ran eighteen months ago. The biggest uplift per hour invested: write one SLO with burn-rate alerts — it will force structure on everything else.

## Per-Dimension Assessment

### D1. SLOs & SLIs — 0/4 (None)

No SLO/SLI files in the repo. Customer contracts mention uptime targets; these are not represented operationally.

**Gaps:**
- No SLO spec committed.
- No measurement of success rate as a first-class metric.
- No burn-rate alerts.

### D2. Runbooks — 2/4 (Defined)

Fourteen runbooks in `runbooks/`. Four are linked from Alertmanager; the other ten are orphaned. Three runbooks last updated > 365 days; their linked alerts reference endpoints that were deprecated.

**Gaps:**
- Runbook freshness SLA unset.
- Most runbooks lack a "first step" executable command.
- No auto-generation of ticket when an alert fires with no linked runbook.

### D3. On-call — 1/4 (Ad-hoc)

No rotation documented. Three engineers self-assign during business hours via a Slack channel. No written escalation policy. PagerDuty is used but the schedule is "everyone everyday" — meaning no-one is specifically accountable.

**Gaps:**
- Single-person oncall risk.
- No response-time target.
- Burnout risk — not measurable because no rotation.

### D4. Postmortems — 2/4 (Defined)

Three postmortems under `docs/postmortems/`, each using a different template. Action items are mentioned in prose but not tracked in Jira; two out of three review dates were missed.

**Gaps:**
- No canonical template (recommend the Anthril template).
- No action-item tracker.
- No cross-team postmortem review cadence.

### D5. Game days — 1/4 (Ad-hoc)

One documented game day from October 2024 that found three gaps. Two gaps fixed; one open. No subsequent game day.

**Gaps:**
- No cadence set.
- No game-day runbook.
- Fixes not tracked to completion.
